Making a Feminist Internet in Africa: A Reflection

by Bahle Jacqueline (Jackie) Sibanda on 20th April 2025

This week's reflection examines 'Making a Feminist Internet in Africa' by Magenya, which challenges the notion of the internet as a neutral space and explores how African feminists are reimagining digital spaces for greater inclusion.

THE MYTH OF A NEUTRAL INTERNET

Magenya dismantles the idea that the internet is an equal playing field, showing how it replicates offline inequalities - particularly for African women, queer communities, and people with disabilities. The COVID-19 pandemic revealed how the push to move everything online ignored existing digital sectionides and safety concerns.

BUILDING FEMINIST DIGITAL SPACES

The article highlights the 2019 Making a Feminist Internet in Africa gathering, where over 50 feminists developed principles for creating more just digital spaces. These principles position African feminists as crucial architects of an internet that centers marginalized voices rather than dominant narratives.

AFRICA AS CREATOR, NOT JUST CONSUMER

Magenya critiques how African innovation is often framed only as crisis response. She envisions an internet that showcases the full spectrum of African life - including joy, creativity, and freedom - particularly for queer people and disabled women who are frequently excluded from digital narratives.

UNANSWERED QUESTIONS AND CALL TO ACTION

While inspiring, the article leaves room to explore practical barriers to access. However, Magenya's core message remains powerful: a feminist internet requires creating digital spaces rooted in safety, care, and genuine representation - going far beyond simple connectivity.

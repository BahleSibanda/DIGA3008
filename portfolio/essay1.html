UX And UI Analysis: A Case Study of the South African Department of Home Affairs Website

by Bahle Jacqueline (Jackie) Sibanda on 20th April 2025

User Experience (UX) design plays a crucial role in creating digital platforms that are both effective and inclusive. This analysis examines the South African Department of Home Affairs (DHA) website as a case study for ethical UX design in government services.

THE IMPORTANCE OF ETHICAL UX IN GOVERNMENT SERVICES

The DHA website serves as a key portal for essential services including passport applications, ID renewals, and visa processes. As a platform handling sensitive biometric data, it must balance functionality with ethical considerations like accessibility and data privacy under South Africa's POPIA regulations.

NAVIGATION AND VISUAL HIERARCHY ISSUES

The site's hamburger menu frequently overlaps with warning banners on mobile devices, creating accessibility barriers. Interactive elements lack consistency - with varying button styles (rounded orange vs square white) that confuse rather than guide users. As noted by Babich (2025), inconsistent visual language forces users to decode interface elements rather than intuitively understanding them.

ACCESSIBILITY SHORTCOMINGS

Automated testing reveals 23 accessibility violations including poor contrast ratios and missing alt text. The non-responsive design creates particular problems for mobile users, with navigation elements extending off-screen. These issues disproportionately affect users with visual impairments or limited digital literacy.

ETHICAL CONCERNS IN DATA PRACTICES

The website fails basic transparency requirements, offering no clear privacy policy or cookie consent options. This violates the principle of informed consent and undermines trust in how sensitive personal data is handled - a critical concern for a government service managing identity documents.

CONCLUSION

While the DHA website delivers functionally, its UX shortcomings create unnecessary barriers and raise ethical concerns. Public sector digital services must prioritize inclusive design to ensure equitable access to essential services for all citizens.
